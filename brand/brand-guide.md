## 1. Brand snapshot

**MyWealthLens** is a South African personal finance and wealth-calculator web app at [mywealthlens.co.za](https://mywealthlens.co.za) — the canonical SA domain. The `.com` domain currently redirects to the same project but is reserved for a future US-market expansion (Stripe payments, USD pricing) and should **not** be used in SA-targeted copy. Always link `mywealthlens.co.za` in social posts and ads aimed at a SA audience.

The product helps South Africans answer the financial questions they actually have — "How much do I need to retire?", "What return rate do I need to hit my goal?", "Am I saving enough?" — using a fast, focused, SA-specific calculator and free educational resources.

The product is built around the **Goal Solver**: instead of telling you only what your money will grow to, you pick the variable you want answered — initial capital, monthly contribution, or annual return — adjust the other two, and the calculator solves for your chosen variable in real time.

### Free vs Pro

| Tier | What you get |
|---|---|
| **Free** (sign-up required) | Goal Solver, Projection, and TFSA modes; full summary statistics |
| **Pro** (R39/month or R399/year) | Adds: Forecast After Target (retirement income drawdown), Inflation-Adjusted Projections, 3-Scenario Comparison, Tax Analysis (CGT + dividends withholding), Export to PDF |

## 2. What we exist to do

**Mission:** Give South Africans the math they need to make confident financial decisions, without paying for advice they don't need.

**Why it matters:** Most South Africans either (a) don't engage with their finances because the tools and language are intimidating, or (b) over-rely on financial advisors with conflicts of interest. MyWealthLens is for the people in between — financially curious, capable of making their own decisions, but currently doing the math on a napkin.

## 3. Target audience

Three primary personas. Use these as mental models when generating content.

### Persona A — "The Curious Saver" (largest segment)
- 28–42 years old, urban professional, R20k–R60k take-home/month
- Has a TFSA, possibly an RA, maybe some unit trusts
- Wants to know: "Am I on track? Should I save more? What return do I actually need?"
- Pain point: Spreadsheets are intimidating; advisors feel like a sales pitch
- Reads on: LinkedIn, Instagram, TikTok, Twitter/X, YouTube finance channels
- Hooks they respond to: clear numbers, real SA examples, calculator screenshots

### Persona B — "The Self-Directed Investor"
- 35–55 years old, financially literate, manages own portfolio
- Has EasyEquities, OUTvest, Sygnia, or similar low-cost platforms
- Wants to know: "What's my realistic FIRE number?", "How does inflation actually erode this?", "Am I optimising tax-efficiently?"
- Pain point: Wants control; distrusts vague advice
- Reads on: LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Reddit (r/PersonalFinanceZA), niche newsletters
- Hooks they respond to: behavioural insights, tax math, portfolio-construction logic

### Persona C — "The FIRE Aspirer"
- 25–40 years old, ambitious, wants financial independence
- Reading r/financialindependence, listening to podcasts like Money Mailbag, Honest Money
- Wants to know: "How early can I retire?", "How aggressive must I be?", "What if returns are lower?"
- Pain point: Loose advice; needs concrete projections to plan around
- Reads on: Twitter/X, YouTube, Reddit, Substack
- Hooks they respond to: scenario modelling, sensitivity to assumptions, FI math

## 5. Brand voice

### Do
- Be **direct**: "Here's the math." Not "Allow me to walk you through some considerations…"
- Be **specific with numbers**: "Save R3,200/month at 9% real return → R5.2m in 25 years" beats "save more and you'll retire well"
- Be **honest about uncertainty**: "Returns are never guaranteed; here's how the math sits at different assumptions"
- Be **SA-rooted**: reference Reg 28, two-pot, TFSA limits, repo rate, JSE — these are signals you understand the user's world
- Be **respectful of the reader's intelligence**: assume they can handle a 9% return assumption being explained

### Don't
- Don't sound like a financial advisor pitch. No "your dreams", "secure your future", "let our experts guide you"
- Don't promise outcomes. "Build wealth in 5 years" is a no — too close to a regulated promise
- Don't use generic finance clichés: "money makes money", "compound interest is the 8th wonder" (overused)
- Don't lecture. Don't moralise about spending habits. The audience is adults
- Don't over-emoji. One or two strategic ones (📈 📊 🇿🇦) per post max

### Reference phrases
- ✅ "What's your number? We'll find it."
- ✅ "Stop guessing. Start solving."
- ✅ "Built for South Africa."
- ✅ "The math you should already know."
- ❌ "Take control of your financial destiny"
- ❌ "Let your money work harder for you"

## 7. Content pillars

Rotate posts across these five pillars. Aim for ~20% per pillar over a month so the feed isn't repetitive.

### Pillar 1 — "The math you should already know"
*Educational, often-counterintuitive financial mathematics.*

Examples:
- The real vs nominal return distinction — why 12% returns at 6% inflation are 5.7%, not 6%
- The cost of a 1% fee over 30 years (it's not 30%, it's ~26% of the final value)
- Why doubling your contribution rate doesn't double your retirement — and what does
- How long it takes to recover from a 30% drawdown (it's not 30% return)
- The R-value of one missed year of TFSA contributions over 40 years

### Pillar 2 — "Practical SA scenarios"
*Concrete examples using real SA constraints — TFSA, RA, two-pot, etc.*

Examples:
- "TFSA vs RA — which should you max first if you only have R3,000/month?"
- "How much you need in SA to retire on R30k/month for life"
- "Two-pot retirement system: how it changes your withdrawal math"
- "Why most SA retirees outlive their savings (and what number prevents it)"
- "The R46k TFSA limit — what 30 years of maxing it actually builds"

### Pillar 3 — "Goal Solver in action"
*Demos of the actual tool solving real questions. Screenshots + caption.*

Examples:
- "What return do you need to hit R5m in 20 years saving R3k/month? Open the calculator → answer in 3 seconds."
- Before/after showing a goal moving from "impossible" to "doable" by tweaking contribution
- Sensitivity comparison: "Same goal, two return assumptions — see the gap"
- Reverse-engineering a celebrity's wealth claim: "Was it really self-made?"

### Pillar 4 — "Behavioural finance, SA flavour"
*Cognitive biases and habits costing South Africans money.*

Examples:
- Recency bias and JSE drawdowns
- Why "saving for a rainy day" is not the same as "investing"
- The cost of loyalty bonuses on bank accounts (vs moving the money)
- Lifestyle inflation when salary grows — the silent retirement killer
- The vehicle finance trap and what the same money invested would do

### Pillar 5 — "Behind the build / personal"
*Founder-voice content. Builds trust and humanises the product.*

Examples:
- Why I built this (frustration with calculators that solve only one direction)
- How the Goal Solver works under the hood (light technical, builds credibility)
- "Tested by [N] users this week — here's what they're solving for"
- Pricing decisions explained (R39 because: not free, not a rip-off)

## 11. Compliance guardrails (CRITICAL)

MyWealthLens is a **calculator and education tool**, NOT a registered financial advisor (FAIS). All marketing copy must:

### Always include or imply
- This is a tool / calculator, not advice
- Past performance does not guarantee future results
- Returns are illustrative, not promised

### Never say
- ❌ "We will grow your money"
- ❌ "Guaranteed returns"
- ❌ "Beat the market"
- ❌ "[Specific fund/product] is the best"
- ❌ "You will retire with R[X]" (in promised tone)
- ❌ Recommend specific stocks, funds, ETFs, unit trusts, or platforms by name in a "buy this" framing
- ❌ Anything that could be interpreted as advice for a specific person's circumstances

### Always say (or have a version of) at the bottom of any post making projections
- "Returns illustrative — not guaranteed"
- "For information only. Not financial advice."
- "Past performance ≠ future returns."

### Comparison content
You may **compare publicly available products by their stated features** (e.g. "TFSA annual limit is R46k; RA limit is 27.5% of income up to R350k") but never editorialise which is "better" without the disclaimer that it depends on individual circumstances.

## 16. What NOT to post

- Specific stock picks or "buy now" content
- Negative content about specific competitors (financial advisors, platforms, products)
- Anything that could be interpreted as advice for a specific person's situation
- Political content, particularly around fiscal policy unless purely educational
- "Hustle culture" content — we respect the reader's existing life decisions
- Engagement bait ("Comment YES if you want to be wealthy")
- Influencer-style "I made R[X] this month" claims of any kind

## 17. Quick-reference for AI agents

If you're an AI generating a post, before you submit, check:

1. **Voice check**: Does it sound like a friendly numerate friend, not a financial advisor? ✅
2. **Specificity check**: Does it have at least one concrete number? ✅
3. **Compliance check**: Have you avoided promising outcomes / recommending specific products? ✅
4. **CTA check**: Is there a clear path to mywealthlens.co.za? ✅
5. **Disclaimer check**: If projections are involved, did you note "not financial advice / illustrative"? ✅
6. **Platform fit check**: Does the length, tone, and hashtag count match the platform conventions? ✅
7. **Promo check**: Have you avoided mentioning promo codes (e.g. `FIRSTMONTH19`)? Promos are an **email-nurture-only** lever — never surface them in organic social. ✅

If yes to all seven, post.

## 18. Channel integration — organic vs email nurture

Organic social and the email nurture sequence run in parallel and should **complement, not duplicate** each other.

### Email nurture (private, owned channel)
- 8-step sequence to free signups via `marketing_subscribers`: Day 0 → 3 → 7 → 14 → 21 → 30 → 60 → 90.
- Day 60/90 only fire to subscribers who opened ≤1 prior email (gated by Resend open-tracking webhook).
- Promo codes (e.g. `FIRSTMONTH19` for R19 first month) are introduced **here, not in organic content**. Keeps the email funnel valuable and avoids cheapening the R39 price anchor publicly.
- All sends from `noreply@mywealthlens.co.za` with `Reply-To: support@mywealthlens.co.za`.

### Organic social (public, top-of-funnel)
- Job is to drive traffic to mywealthlens.co.za, where the visitor either:
  (a) opens the free calculator, or
  (b) submits a scenario email — entering the nurture sequence above.
- Should establish credibility + showcase the Goal Solver. **No promo codes**, no "limited-time" pricing language.
- Content pillars (§7) are the menu; rotate ~20% per pillar over a month.

### Anti-duplication rule
If a specific number/insight has already gone out in a nurture email, organic content can reference the same *topic* but should reframe with a different angle, hook, or example. The reader who signed up for both channels should not feel they're getting the same content twice.

### Funnel signal back to brand voice
The fact that we have an 8-step nurture sequence telling people "stop guessing, start solving" reinforces the brand promise. Organic posts can implicitly trust the reader to make their own decisions because the long-form education exists in the funnel — keep social posts punchy, not lecturing.
